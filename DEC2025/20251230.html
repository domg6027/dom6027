The Torah distinguishes between what is lawful and what is just. A claim may be permitted by law, yet still be unrighteous if it ignores mercy, fairness, or the weight of consequences. YHVH calls His people not merely to assert rights, but to pursue justice that reflects His character.

Just claims are restrained, measured, and accountable. They do not exploit technicalities, power, or vulnerability. They seek restoration rather than advantage, truth rather than victory, and peace rather than dominance.
