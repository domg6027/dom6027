📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Chamishi, Chamishi 8, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Respect for Inherited Order

📘 READING

“You shall not remove your neighbour's boundary marker, which the men of old have set, in your inheritance which you inherit in the land that YHVH your Elohim is giving you to possess.”
- Debarim (Deuteronomy) 19:14

“Do not remove the ancient boundary, nor enter the fields of the fatherless.”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 23:10

“Let all things be done decently and in order.”
- Qorintiyim Aleph (1 Corinthians) 14:40

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH established boundaries not only to define property but also to preserve order, justice and stability within His people. The ancient boundary markers represented an inheritance entrusted from one generation to the next. To move them was to disregard both the rights of others and the order that YHVH had established.

This mitzvah reminds us that respecting inherited order is an expression of faithfulness. Whether in our families, our communities or our walk with YHVH, we honour Him when we preserve what is righteous rather than altering it for personal advantage or convenience.

🩵 REFLECTION

Modern culture often encourages people to discard long-established principles in favour of whatever seems new or beneficial. YHVH calls us instead to examine what has been faithfully handed down and to preserve everything that is consistent with His Word.

May we value the righteous foundations that YHVH has established. As we respect His order, guard His truth and faithfully pass it on to those who follow us, we become faithful stewards of the inheritance He has entrusted to His people.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for establishing an order that reflects Your wisdom, justice and faithfulness. Teach us to respect the righteous boundaries and principles You have given, and guard us from changing them for personal gain or convenience. Help us to preserve Your truth, live with integrity and pass Your ways faithfully to future generations. May our lives always honour Your holy Name.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
