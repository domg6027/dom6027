🕯️ SHABBAT MESSAGE, Chamishi 3, 6028 AA 🕯️

Shalom, Mitspacha!

✨ THEMES

Justice - Life Is Not for Sale / Kingship and Obedience - Shemu'el Aleph (1 Samuel)

👐 OPENING PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for bringing us safely into Your holy Shabbat. We lay aside the burdens and concerns of the past week and enter Your appointed rest with grateful hearts. Open our minds to receive Your instruction, strengthen our faith through Your Word, and renew our desire to walk faithfully in Your covenant. May this Shabbat draw us closer to You and to one another as we worship You in spirit and in truth.

🍷 COMMUNION

As we partake of the bread, we remember Yahusha HaMashiach, the Bread of Life, whose body was given for us.

As we drink from the cup, we remember the renewed covenant established through His blood, giving thanks for the forgiveness, reconciliation, and hope we have through Him.

May this communion strengthen our commitment to walk faithfully in obedience to YHVH's Torah and to live as ambassadors of His Kingdom until Messiah returns.

📜 TORAH READING

“You shall take no ransom for the life of a murderer who is guilty of death, but he shall surely be put to death.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:31

“You shall not pollute the land in which you are, for blood defiles the land; and no atonement can be made for the land for the blood that is shed on it, except by the blood of him who shed it.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:33

📖 MESSAGE

YHVH teaches that human life is sacred and cannot be reduced to a financial value. The prohibition against accepting a ransom for a deliberate murderer demonstrates that justice must never be bought, negotiated or influenced by wealth, status or personal advantage. A human life is not a commodity, and the value of justice cannot be measured in silver or gold.

The holiness of life is inseparable from the holiness of YHVH's justice. When deliberate murder is treated as something that can simply be compensated for, the value of the life taken is diminished and the righteousness of the land is defiled. YHVH therefore establishes a standard in which justice cannot be sold to the highest bidder.

The book of Shemu'el Aleph reveals another dimension of this truth: kingship itself is not a licence for independence from YHVH. A king may possess authority, influence and power, but he remains subject to the Word of YHVH. Sha'ul's failure demonstrates that obedience cannot be replaced by position, sacrifice or outward religious appearance. YHVH desires obedience rather than the assumption that human authority may redefine His commands.

These themes are deeply connected. Justice cannot be bought, and obedience cannot be negotiated. Whether a person possesses no authority or rules over a nation, every human being remains accountable to YHVH. Wealth cannot purchase righteousness, power cannot cancel obedience, and position cannot place anyone above the standards of the Elohim who gives life and establishes justice.

As followers of Yahusha HaMashiach, we are called to honour the sanctity of life, uphold justice without partiality and remain obedient to YHVH regardless of our position or circumstances. True authority is demonstrated not by the ability to impose one's will, but by the willingness to submit to the will of YHVH.

💙 APPLICATION

Examine your own heart this Shabbat. Are there areas where personal advantage, influence or convenience has been allowed to compete with truth and obedience? Have you ever treated something as negotiable that YHVH has clearly established?

Choose today to remember that neither life nor righteousness is for sale. Let your decisions reflect the value YHVH places on human life, and let your obedience demonstrate that no position, desire or personal benefit is more important than faithfulness to His Word.

👐 CLOSING PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for teaching us that human life is sacred and that justice cannot be bought. Help us to honour every life created in Your image and to reject every form of corruption, partiality and compromise. Teach us also that all authority remains subject to You, and strengthen us to obey Your Word rather than relying upon position, influence or outward appearances. May our lives reflect Your righteousness, wisdom and truth, and may we remain faithful to Your covenant through Yahusha HaMashiach.

We pray for peace in the Middle East, South Africa and throughout the world. In this time of continued tension and uncertainty, especially in and around Yisrael and its neighbouring nations, bring restraint where conflict threatens to grow, grant wisdom to leaders, protect innocent lives and establish Your righteousness and lasting peace according to Your perfect will.

We pray for an end-time revival. Awaken hearts across every nation, call people to repentance, restore a deep love for Your truth and raise up faithful servants who will proclaim the Good News of Your Kingdom with boldness and humility.

We pray for the elderly, especially those who suffer from loneliness, neglect or abuse. Surround them with loving families, compassionate caregivers and faithful believers who will honour them with dignity, kindness and practical support.

We pray for farmers and all who labour to provide food for their communities. Protect them from violence, strengthen them through every hardship, bless the work of their hands and grant favourable seasons that will produce abundant harvests.

We pray for those affected by wars, natural disasters and humanitarian crises. Comfort those who grieve, heal the injured, provide for the displaced and restore hope to those whose lives have been shaken by suffering and loss.

We pray for evangelists, teachers, missionaries and all faithful servants who proclaim Your truth throughout the world. Strengthen them with wisdom, courage and endurance, protect them where persecution is increasing and open doors for the Good News to reach many hearts.

We pray for those who are ill, especially those battling cancer, chronic diseases and emerging viral outbreaks. Stretch out Your healing hand, guide those who provide medical care, strengthen families walking alongside them and fill every heart with hope and peace.

We pray concerning the coming tribulation and the signs unfolding before our eyes. Grant Your people discernment, steadfast faith and spiritual maturity. Help us to remain watchful, prepared and unwavering as we await the return of Yahusha HaMashiach.

We lift up Derech Olam Ministries before You. Direct every decision according to Your perfect will, provide every resource needed for the work You have entrusted to us, open doors that no one can shut, bless every ministry platform and let Your Name alone receive all honour and glory.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shabbat Shalom!
