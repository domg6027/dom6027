🕯️ SHABBAT MESSAGE, REVI'I 25, 6028 AA 🕯️

Shalom, Mitspacha!

✨ THEMES

Distinction between Accidental and Intentional Killing / Cycles of Faithfulness - Shophetim (Judges)

👐 OPENING PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for bringing us safely into Your holy Shabbat. We lay aside the burdens of the past week and enter Your appointed rest with grateful hearts. Open our minds to receive Your instruction, strengthen our faith through Your Word, and renew our desire to walk faithfully in Your covenant. May this Shabbat draw us closer to You and to one another as we worship You in spirit and in truth.

🍷 COMMUNION

As we partake of the bread, we remember Yahusha HaMashiach, the Bread of Life, whose body was given for us. As we drink from the cup, we remember the renewed covenant established through His blood, giving thanks for the forgiveness, reconciliation and hope we have through Him. May this communion strengthen our commitment to walk faithfully in obedience to YHVH's Torah and to live as ambassadors of His Kingdom until Messiah returns.

📜 TORAH READING

“Then YHVH spoke to Mosheh, saying, ‘Speak to the children of Yisrael, and say to them: When you cross the Yarden into the land of Kena'an, then you shall appoint cities to be cities of refuge for you, that the manslayer who kills any person unintentionally may flee there.’”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:9-11

“But if he struck him with an iron object, so that he died, he is a murderer; the murderer shall surely be put to death.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:16

“In those days there was no king in Yisrael; everyone did what was right in his own eyes.”
- Shophetim (Judges) 21:25

📖 MESSAGE

The Torah carefully distinguishes between accidental killing and intentional murder. YHVH commanded that every case be examined with fairness so that justice would never be driven by anger, revenge, or assumption. Those who caused death unintentionally were granted refuge until a proper judgment could be made, while deliberate murder demanded righteous accountability. This demonstrates that YHVH's justice is always balanced by wisdom, truth, and mercy.

The book of Shophetim reveals what happens when people abandon this standard. Repeatedly, Yisrael turned away from YHVH, experienced oppression, cried out for deliverance, and was rescued through judges whom YHVH raised up. Yet each cycle ended with the people drifting back into disobedience because they failed to remain steadfast in covenant faithfulness.

These two themes complement one another. Justice requires careful discernment rather than impulsive judgment, while faithfulness requires continual obedience rather than temporary repentance during times of crisis. Both remind us that YHVH desires hearts that remain steadfast in every season, not only when circumstances become difficult.

As followers of Messiah, we are called to seek truth before judging others, to exercise mercy without compromising righteousness, and to remain faithful regardless of the changing conditions around us. When we walk in covenant obedience each day, we break the destructive cycles of unfaithfulness and become living testimonies of YHVH's enduring righteousness.

💙 APPLICATION

Examine your own heart this Shabbat. Are there areas where quick judgments have replaced careful discernment? Have there been seasons where faithfulness has depended upon circumstances rather than upon your covenant with YHVH?

Choose today to pursue both justice and steadfast obedience. Trust YHVH to guide your decisions with wisdom, and commit yourself anew to walking faithfully before Him each day, allowing your life to reflect His righteousness, mercy, and truth.

👐 CLOSING PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for teaching us that true justice is rooted in wisdom and that faithful obedience is the pathway to lasting blessing. Guard our hearts from careless judgment, strengthen us to remain steadfast through every season of life, and fill us with the discernment that comes from Your Spirit. May we reflect Your righteousness, extend Your mercy, and walk faithfully before You all the days of our lives. Prepare us continually for the coming of Your Kingdom and keep us firmly established in Your covenant through Yahusha HaMashiach.

We pray for peace in the Middle East, South Africa and throughout the world. In this time of continued tension and uncertainty, especially in and around Israel and its neighbouring nations, bring restraint where conflict threatens to grow, grant wisdom to leaders, protect innocent lives and establish Your righteousness and lasting peace according to Your perfect will.

We pray for an end-time revival. Awaken hearts across every nation, call people to repentance, restore a deep love for Your truth and raise up faithful servants who will proclaim the Good News of Your Kingdom with boldness and humility.

We pray for the elderly, especially those who suffer from loneliness, neglect or abuse. Surround them with loving families, compassionate caregivers and faithful believers who will honour them with dignity, kindness and practical support.

We pray for farmers and all who labour to provide food for their communities. Protect them from violence, strengthen them through every hardship, bless the work of their hands and grant favourable seasons that will produce abundant harvests.

We pray for those affected by wars, natural disasters and humanitarian crises. Comfort those who grieve, heal the injured, provide for the displaced and restore hope to those whose lives have been shaken by suffering and loss.

We pray for evangelists, teachers, missionaries and all faithful servants who proclaim Your truth throughout the world. Strengthen them with wisdom, courage and endurance, protect them where persecution is increasing and open doors for the Good News to reach many hearts.

We pray for those who are ill, especially those battling cancer, chronic diseases and emerging viral outbreaks. Stretch out Your healing hand, guide those who provide medical care, strengthen families walking alongside them and fill every heart with hope and peace.

We pray concerning the coming tribulation and the signs unfolding before our eyes. Grant Your people discernment, steadfast faith and spiritual maturity. Help us to remain watchful, prepared and unwavering as we await the return of Yahusha HaMashiach.

We lift up Derech Olam Ministries before You. Direct every decision according to Your perfect will, provide every resource needed for the work You have entrusted to us, open doors that no one can shut, bless every ministry platform and let Your Name alone receive all honour and glory.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shabbat Shalom!
