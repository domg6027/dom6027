📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Erev Shabbat, Revi'i 24, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

As we prepare our hearts for the approaching Shabbat, let us complete our work with thanksgiving and look forward to the rest that YHVH has appointed for His people. May this preparation day remind us to set aside both time and our hearts for worship, fellowship, and renewed devotion to our heavenly Father.

⭐ THEME

Cities of Refuge – Purpose

📘 READING

“You shall prepare roads for yourself, and divide into three parts the territory of your land which YHVH your Elohim is giving you to inherit, that any manslayer may flee there.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 19:3

“And this is the case of the manslayer who flees there, that he may live: whoever kills his neighbour unintentionally, not having hated him in time past.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 19:4

“YHVH is good, a stronghold in the day of trouble; and He knows those who trust in Him.”
- Nachum (Nahum) 1:7

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

The cities of refuge reveal both the justice and the mercy of YHVH. While deliberate murder required righteous judgment, those responsible for accidental death were provided with a place of safety until their case could be heard fairly. In this way, the Torah protected innocent lives from revenge while ensuring that justice was carefully administered.

These cities also point us to the compassion of Elohim, who provides refuge for those who seek Him sincerely. Though earthly cities offered temporary protection, YHVH Himself remains the ultimate refuge for all who trust in Him. His justice is never separated from His mercy, and His mercy never ignores His righteousness.

🩵 REFLECTION

Life often reminds us that justice requires patience, wisdom, and careful discernment. Before reaching conclusions, we should seek the truth, listen fairly, and allow righteousness rather than emotion to guide our decisions.

As we prepare to enter Shabbat, let us also find our refuge in YHVH. In His presence we discover peace, forgiveness, strength, and confidence to continue walking faithfully in His covenant.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for revealing both justice and mercy through Your Torah. Thank You for being our refuge, our protector, and our righteous Judge. Teach us to seek truth carefully, extend mercy wisely, and uphold justice faithfully in every circumstance. As we prepare for Your holy Shabbat, quiet our hearts, strengthen our faith, and draw us closer to You. May we always find safety, peace, and hope in Your presence as we walk in joyful obedience to Your Word.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
