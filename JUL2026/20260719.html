📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Rishon, Chamishi 4, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Blood Avenger - Limits and Restraint

📘 READING

“The avenger of blood himself shall put the murderer to death; when he meets him, he shall put him to death.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:19

“But if he pushed him suddenly without enmity, or threw anything at him without lying in wait... then the congregation shall judge between the slayer and the avenger of blood according to these right-rulings.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:22,24

“Do not say, ‘I shall do to him as he has done to me; I shall render to the man according to his work.’”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 24:29

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH recognised the deep desire for justice after the loss of a loved one, yet He placed clear limits upon the actions of the blood avenger. Justice was never to be driven by uncontrolled emotion or personal vengeance. Every case had to be examined carefully so that truth, fairness and righteousness would prevail.

This mitzvah teaches that righteous restraint is just as important as righteous action. YHVH calls His people to submit their emotions to His instruction, ensuring that justice is exercised according to His standards rather than human anger.

🩵 REFLECTION

Our natural response to being wronged is often to seek immediate retaliation. Yet YHVH reminds us that wisdom requires patience, careful discernment and self-control. Acting in anger can easily lead to further injustice, while waiting upon YHVH allows truth to prevail.

May we learn to restrain our emotions, seek righteous judgment and trust YHVH to establish justice in His perfect way and in His perfect time.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for teaching us that justice must always be guided by truth and not by uncontrolled emotion. Give us patient hearts, sound judgment and the humility to submit every situation to Your wisdom. Help us to reject personal vengeance and to pursue righteousness according to Your Word. May our lives reflect Your justice, mercy and self-control in every circumstance.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
