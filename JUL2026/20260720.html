📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Sheni, Chamishi 5, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Preventing Cycles of Violence

📘 READING

“These shall be for you a law of right-ruling throughout your generations in all your dwellings.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:29

“So the congregation shall deliver the slayer from the hand of the avenger of blood, and the congregation shall return him to the city of refuge where he had fled.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:25

“Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.”
- Romiyim (Romans) 12:21

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH established the cities of refuge and the process of righteous judgment to prevent violence from escalating into endless cycles of revenge. By requiring every case to be examined carefully and judged impartially, He protected both the innocent and the integrity of justice. His instructions restrained human anger and ensured that justice remained under His righteous authority.

This mitzvah reminds us that peace is preserved when justice is guided by truth rather than emotion. YHVH calls His people to break the cycle of retaliation by trusting His righteous judgments and walking according to His Torah.

🩵 REFLECTION

Unresolved anger often leads to further conflict, while patience and obedience create opportunities for reconciliation and peace. Although justice must never be ignored, it must always be exercised according to YHVH's standards rather than our emotions.

May we become peacemakers who refuse to fuel conflict with revenge or bitterness. As we choose forgiveness where appropriate, pursue truth and trust YHVH's justice, we become instruments of His peace in a troubled world.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for establishing righteous justice that protects both truth and peace. Guard our hearts against anger, bitterness and revenge. Teach us to pursue reconciliation where possible, to uphold justice with integrity and to trust You when situations are beyond our control. May our lives reflect Your wisdom, mercy and peace in every relationship.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
