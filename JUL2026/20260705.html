📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Rishon, Revi'i 19, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Measure-for-Measure Justice

📘 READING

“You shall do to him as he thought to have done to his brother; so you shall put away the evil from among you.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 19:19

“With the merciful You will show Yourself merciful; with a blameless man You will show Yourself blameless.”
- Tehillim (Psalms) 18:25

“For with what judgment you judge, you will be judged; and with the measure you use, it will be measured back to you.”
- Mattityahu (Matthew) 7:2

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH's justice is perfectly balanced. Throughout the Torah, He establishes the principle that those who seek to harm others through wickedness should themselves bear the consequences of their own actions. This measure-for-measure justice protects the innocent, restrains evil, and upholds righteousness within the covenant community.

Messiah reaffirmed this principle by teaching that the standards we apply to others will also be applied to us. This is not merely a warning, but an invitation to practise fairness, mercy, and integrity. As recipients of YHVH's grace, we are called to judge righteously, act humbly, and extend the same justice that reflects His holy character.

🩵 REFLECTION

Every choice we make reveals the measure by which we live. When we respond with honesty, compassion, and righteousness, we demonstrate trust in YHVH's perfect justice rather than relying on our own desires or emotions.

Today, let us examine the standards we apply to others. May our words, decisions, and actions reflect the fairness and mercy we ourselves hope to receive from our heavenly Father.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for Your perfect justice, which is always righteous, impartial, and true. Teach us to measure others with the same mercy, integrity, and fairness that You desire from us. Guard us from hypocrisy, pride, and harsh judgment. Fill our hearts with humility, wisdom, and compassion so that every decision we make may honour Your Name. May our lives reflect the justice and mercy of Messiah as we faithfully walk in obedience to Your Torah.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
