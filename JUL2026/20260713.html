📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Sheni, Revi'i 27, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Exile of the Accidental Manslayer

📘 READING

“He shall dwell in that city until he stands before the congregation for judgment, and until the death of the high priest who is in those days.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:12

“He shall remain in that city of refuge until the death of the high priest. After the death of the high priest the manslayer may return to the land of his possession.”
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 35:28

“For whom YHVH loves He corrects, even as a father the son in whom he delights.”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 3:12

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

The Torah required that a person who caused another's death unintentionally remain in a city of refuge until the death of the high priest. Although innocent of murder, the manslayer still lived with the consequences of his actions. This command upheld both justice and mercy, protecting life while acknowledging the seriousness of taking human life.

YHVH teaches that our actions often carry lasting consequences, even when there is no evil intent. His discipline is never meant to destroy us but to shape our character, deepen our humility and teach us wisdom. Within His justice, mercy always provides a path toward restoration.

🩵 REFLECTION

Every believer experiences moments when mistakes bring consequences that cannot simply be undone. Rather than becoming discouraged, we should receive these moments as opportunities to grow in wisdom, patience and dependence upon YHVH.

May we accept His loving discipline with humble hearts, trusting that His purposes are always good. As we walk faithfully before Him, He transforms even our failures into testimonies of His grace, righteousness and redeeming love.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for Your perfect justice that is always joined with mercy. Teach us to accept correction with humility, to learn from our mistakes and to trust Your wisdom when we face the consequences of our actions. Shape our hearts through Your discipline, strengthen our faith and lead us continually in the path of righteousness. May our lives bear witness to Your restoring grace and steadfast love.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
