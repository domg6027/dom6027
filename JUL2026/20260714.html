📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Shlishi, Revi'i 28, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Boundaries as Protection

📘 READING

“You shall not remove your neighbour's landmark, which the men of old have set, in your inheritance which you shall inherit in the land that YHVH your Elohim is giving you to possess.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 19:14

“Remove not the ancient landmark which your fathers have set.”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 22:28

“Your word I have treasured in my heart, that I might not sin against You.”
- Tehillim (Psalms) 119:11

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH commanded His people not to move the ancient boundary markers that defined each family's inheritance. These landmarks protected property, prevented injustice and preserved the order He had established within Yisrael. Respecting these boundaries was an act of integrity and obedience, acknowledging that every inheritance ultimately belonged to YHVH.

The same principle applies to our spiritual lives. YHVH has established righteous boundaries through His Torah to protect us from harm and to guide us in paths of life. When we honour His instructions instead of shifting them to suit our own desires, we remain secure within His covenant and experience the blessings of faithful obedience.

🩵 REFLECTION

Modern culture often encourages people to redefine moral boundaries according to personal preference. Yet YHVH's standards remain unchanging because they are rooted in His perfect character rather than human opinion.

May we gladly embrace the boundaries that YHVH has established. They are not barriers to our joy but safeguards for our lives. As we walk within His appointed ways, we discover the freedom, peace and security that come through faithful obedience.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for establishing righteous boundaries that protect and guide Your people. Help us to honour Your Torah without compromise and to resist every temptation to reshape Your truth according to human desires. Write Your Word upon our hearts, strengthen us to walk faithfully within Your covenant and let our lives reflect the wisdom, peace and holiness that come from wholehearted obedience to You.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
