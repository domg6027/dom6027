📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Chamishi, Revi'i 23, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Justice – Strength with Compassion

📘 READING

“Justice, and only justice, you shall pursue, that you may live and inherit the land which YHVH your Elohim is giving you.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 16:20

“He has shown you, O man, what is good; and what does YHVH require of you but to do justly, to love mercy, and to walk humbly with your Elohim?”
- Mikhah (Micah) 6:8

“Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfil the Torah of Messiah.”
- Galatim (Galatians) 6:2

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH never separates justice from compassion. His Torah commands His people to pursue righteousness without compromise while showing mercy to those who are genuinely in need. Justice without compassion becomes harsh, while compassion without justice loses its foundation in truth. Together they reveal the perfect balance of Elohim's character.

Messiah demonstrated both strength and compassion throughout His ministry. He confronted sin without hesitation, yet He welcomed the repentant with mercy and restored those who turned to Him in faith. As His followers, we are called to uphold righteousness while extending grace, reflecting both the holiness and the love of our heavenly Father.

🩵 REFLECTION

Strength is not measured by power alone but by the wisdom to exercise it with humility and compassion. Every opportunity to help another person while remaining faithful to truth reflects the heart of YHVH.

May our lives never compromise justice in the name of kindness, nor abandon compassion in the pursuit of righteousness. As we walk faithfully in both, we become living witnesses of the Kingdom of Elohim.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, thank You for revealing perfect justice and perfect compassion through Your Word. Teach us to stand firmly for righteousness while showing mercy with humble hearts. Give us wisdom to know when to correct, when to comfort, and when to remain silent. May our words, our decisions, and our actions always reflect Your holy character. Strengthen us to walk faithfully in obedience to Your Torah so that others may see Your light through our lives and glorify Your great Name.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
