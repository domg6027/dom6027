🌿 SPECIAL SHABBAT - Pesach 1 - Rishon 15, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Pesach day 1 / Omer 1 / Resting in Deliverance and Walking in Unleavened Life

🤲🏻 Opening Prayer

Blessed are You, YHVH our Elohim, King of the universe, who has brought us into this sacred Shabbat and into the days of Unleavened Bread. Thank You for the rest You provide and for the deliverance You have shown to Your people. May this day be filled with peace, remembrance, and joy in Your presence.

Baruch YHVH.

📖 Message

After the remembrance of last night’s Pesach Seder, this morning invites us into rest — a Double Shabbat set apart for reflection and renewal. We remember that deliverance was not only a moment in the night, but a journey that continued into the morning. Today we pause, breathe, and allow the meaning of redemption to settle deep within our hearts.

The bread without leaven reminds us that change begins within. Just as leaven was removed from the homes of Yisrael, so we are called to walk in sincerity and truth, leaving behind what does not belong in a life set apart. This is a day to rest not only from labor, but from burdens, trusting fully in the faithfulness of YHVH.

Let this Double Shabbat be a time of quiet gratitude. Gather together, reflect on the deliverance remembered last night, and step forward into these days of Unleavened Bread with renewed purpose. Freedom was given so that we might walk differently - lighter, cleaner, and closer to the One who leads us.

🌾 OMER 01 MESSAGE

Today begins the counting of the Omer - the journey from deliverance toward readiness. Each day reminds us that growth takes time, and that preparation follows redemption. Let this first count be a step of intention, marking the beginning of a path that leads toward fullness and purpose.

🤲🏻 Closing Prayer

May this Double Shabbat bring rest to our bodies and peace to our homes. May the remembrance of deliverance strengthen our faith, and may these days of Unleavened Bread guide our steps into a life of sincerity and truth. Let Your presence dwell among us, YHVH, as we rest in the freedom You have given.

Baruch YHVH.
