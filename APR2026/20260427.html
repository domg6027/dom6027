📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Sheni, Sheni 8, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Prohibition of licentiousness

📘 READING

"You do not commit adultery."
- Shemoth (Exodus) 20:14

"And do not be led astray into whoring after your own heart and your own eyes, after which you went whoring."
- Bemidbar (Numbers) 15:39

"How would a young man cleanse his path? To guard it according to Your Word."
- Tehillim (Psalms) 119:9

"For this is the desire of Elohim: your set-apartness, that you should abstain from whoring."
- Tas'loniqim Aleph (1 Thessalonians) 4:3

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

Licentiousness weakens discipline, clouds judgment, and leads hearts away from righteousness. YHVH established clear boundaries to guard His people from falling into patterns that destroy families, corrupt communities, and distance individuals from His presence.

Obedience in matters of purity is not merely personal—it protects households and preserves the integrity of the covenant community. When self-control is practiced and boundaries are respected, honour and stability remain firmly rooted.

Guarding one's conduct reflects reverence for YHVH and respect for others.

🩵 REFLECTION

Temptation often begins quietly, through thoughts, desires, or small compromises that seem harmless at first. Yet small deviations, when ignored, grow into habits that shape character and influence destiny.

Consider the influences that surround your daily life—what you see, what you hear, and what you allow into your thoughts. Strength is built through daily choices that honour righteousness, even when no one else sees.

Purity is preserved through vigilance and faithful obedience.

👐 PRAYER

YHVH, Elohim of holiness, teach us to walk in purity and to guard our hearts with wisdom. Strengthen us to resist temptation and to remain faithful to Your commands. Help us to honour You in our thoughts, our actions, and our relationships. Give us discipline to reject what leads us away from righteousness and courage to walk in truth. Let our lives reflect Your holiness and bring honour to Your Name.

Baruch YHVH.

Aaronitic Blessing

Shalom until tomorrow.
