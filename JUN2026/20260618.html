📰 DAVAR LECHEM, Yom Chamishi, Revi'i 2, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Justice – communal stability

📘 READING

“Justice, justice you shall pursue, that you may live and inherit the land which YHVH your Elohim is giving you.”
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 16:20

“Righteousness exalts a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people.”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 14:34

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

Justice is one of the foundations upon which healthy communities are built. YHVH's commandments establish fairness, honesty, accountability, and respect for one another so that peace and stability may flourish among His people.

When justice is consistently upheld, trust grows within families, congregations, and society. People are able to live securely, knowing that truth is valued above personal advantage and that righteousness governs relationships. Conversely, where justice is ignored or corrupted, fear, division, and instability soon take root.

Every disciple of YHVH contributes to communal stability by choosing integrity in everyday decisions, speaking truthfully, acting fairly, and refusing corruption regardless of personal cost.

🩵 REFLECTION

Communities rarely become unstable overnight. Small compromises with truth and justice gradually weaken the trust that holds people together. Likewise, small acts of righteousness strengthen that trust one decision at a time.

Consider how your own conduct influences those around you. Whether within your family, congregation, workplace, or neighbourhood, every honest word, fair decision, and faithful action contributes to an environment where others may flourish.

May we pursue justice not merely because it is commanded, but because it reflects the righteous character of YHVH and brings stability to those around us.

👐 PRAYER

Abba YHVH, thank You for establishing justice as a foundation for peace and stability. Teach us to walk in integrity, fairness, humility, and truth. Help us to strengthen our families, congregations, and communities through righteous living. Guard us from selfishness, partiality, dishonesty, and compromise, and let our lives reflect Your righteous character in every decision we make.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
