📨 DAVAR LECHEM, Erev Shabbat, Shlishi 18, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Equal treatment of rich and poor

📘 READING

“You shall do no unrighteousness in right-ruling. Do not be partial to the poor or favor the face of the great, but judge your neighbor in righteousness.”
- Wayyiqra (Leviticus) 19:15

“For YHVH your Elohim is Elohim of mighty ones and Master of masters, the great El, mighty and awesome, who shows no partiality nor takes a bribe.”
- Deḇariem (Deuteronomy) 10:17

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH commands that justice be applied equally to all people regardless of wealth, status, influence, position, or circumstance. Neither poverty nor riches should affect righteous judgment. Truth and justice must remain the standard by which matters are evaluated.

The people of YHVH are called to reflect His impartial character. Fair treatment strengthens trust, preserves justice, and demonstrates respect for the dignity and value of every person created by Elohim.

🩵 REFLECTION

Human nature often leans toward favoritism. Some may be tempted to favor the wealthy because of influence or advantage, while others may favor the poor out of sympathy. Scripture teaches that true justice avoids both extremes and evaluates matters according to truth.

YHVH sees beyond social standing, possessions, and reputation. He judges righteously and calls His people to do the same. Consider whether you treat people differently based on their circumstances, appearance, influence, or resources. A heart devoted to YHVH seeks fairness, integrity, and righteousness in every interaction.

👐 PRAYER

Abba YHVH, teach us to walk in fairness, integrity, and righteousness. Help us to treat all people with equal respect and dignity regardless of their status or circumstances. Guard us from favoritism, prejudice, and partiality. Let our judgments reflect Your truth and our actions demonstrate Your justice. Strengthen us to walk humbly before You and to honor every person as one created in Your image.

We pray for peace in the Middle East, South Africa, and the world. Bring stability, righteousness, and divine intervention where conflict rises.

We pray for end-time revival. Let hearts awaken and turn back to Your truth across the earth.

We pray for the elderly, especially those suffering abuse or neglect. Surround them with protection, dignity, care, and comfort.

We pray for the farmers, especially those in danger of attacks. Shield their lives, strengthen their hands, and bless their labor.

We pray for people in war and disaster stricken areas. Comfort those who lost loved ones and heal those who have been injured.

We pray for evangelists across the world, especially where persecution is severe. Empower them with courage, safety, and supernatural doors of opportunity.

We pray for ill people, especially those with cancer and new viral outbreaks. Stretch out Your healing hand and bring restoration and hope.

We pray for the coming tribulation and the signs already appearing. Prepare our hearts with wisdom, endurance, and steadfast faith.

We pray for our ministry, Derech Olam Ministries. Guide each step, open the right doors, and let Your favor rest upon the work.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
