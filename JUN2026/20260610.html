📨 DAVAR LECHEM, Yom Revi'i, Shlishi 23, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Appointment of righteous judges

📘 READING

“Appoint judges and officers in all your gates, which YHVH your Elohim gives you, according to your tribes, and they shall judge the people with righteous right-ruling.”
- Deḇariem (Deuteronomy) 16:18

“You shall not twist right-ruling, you shall not show partiality, nor take a bribe, for the bribe blinds the eyes of the wise and twists the words of the righteous.”
- Deḇariem (Deuteronomy) 16:19

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH commands that judges and leaders be appointed with integrity, wisdom, and righteousness. Those entrusted with authority over others carry the responsibility of upholding justice without corruption, favoritism, or personal gain. Righteous leadership protects communities and preserves stability, fairness, and trust.

The appointment of righteous judges reflects the importance YHVH places upon justice within society. Leadership is not merely a position of influence but a sacred responsibility that requires accountability before Elohim.

🩵 REFLECTION

Throughout history, societies have suffered when leaders and judges acted from greed, corruption, prejudice, or selfish ambition. Unrighteous leadership damages trust and weakens the moral foundation of communities. By contrast, righteous leadership promotes peace, order, and protection for the vulnerable.

Consider how you respond to positions of responsibility in your own life. Whether leading within a family, workplace, ministry, or community, integrity and fairness remain essential. YHVH calls His people not only to desire righteous leadership but also to demonstrate righteousness in the responsibilities entrusted to them.

👐 PRAYER

Abba YHVH, raise up leaders, judges, and authorities who walk in wisdom, integrity, and righteousness. Guard them from corruption, greed, favoritism, and injustice. Teach us also to act faithfully in every responsibility You place before us. Help us to value truth, fairness, and accountability in our decisions and relationships. Let justice be established in our communities, and may our lives reflect Your righteous character in all we do.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
