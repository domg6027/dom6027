📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Shlishi, Revi'i 14, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Justice – Guarding Life and Truth

📘 READING

“You shall not murder.”
- Exodus 20:13

“You shall not bear false witness against your neighbour.”
- Exodus 20:16

“He has shown you, O man, what is good; and what does YHVH require of you but to do justly, to love mercy, and to walk humbly with your Elohim?”
- Micah 6:8

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

Justice is one of the pillars of YHVH's Kingdom. His Torah safeguards both life and truth because neither can flourish where injustice prevails. The commandments against murder and false witness protect the dignity of every person and preserve the integrity of the community.

True justice is more than enforcing laws - it reflects the righteous character of Elohim. Messiah demonstrated that justice is inseparable from mercy, truth, and humility. Those who follow Him are called to defend what is right, reject deception, and value every human life as a gift from the Creator.

🩵 REFLECTION

Every decision we make has the potential to uphold either justice or injustice. Whether in our words, our actions, or our judgments, we honour YHVH when we choose truth over convenience and righteousness over personal gain.

Guarding life and truth begins with the small choices of each day. As we faithfully practise justice in ordinary circumstances, we become trustworthy witnesses of YHVH's Kingdom in a world that desperately needs His light.

👐 PRAYER

Heavenly Father, YHVH, teach us to love justice as You love justice. Help us to protect life, speak truth, and act with integrity in every situation. Give us discernment to recognise deception, courage to stand for what is right, and compassion to extend mercy without compromising righteousness. May our lives reflect Your holy character and bring honour to Your Name in all that we do.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
