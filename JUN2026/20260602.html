📨 DAVAR LECHEM, Yom Shlishi, Shlishi 15, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Clarity of judgment

📘 READING

“You shall do no unrighteousness in right-ruling. Do not be partial to the poor or favor the face of the great, but judge your neighbor in righteousness.”
- Wayyiqra (Leviticus) 19:15

“He who answers a matter before he hears it, it is folly and shame to him.”
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 18:13

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH calls His people to exercise sound and righteous judgment. Clear judgment requires careful listening, honest evaluation of facts, and freedom from prejudice, emotion, favoritism, or assumption. Justice becomes distorted when decisions are made hastily or without full understanding of a matter.

The people of YHVH are instructed to pursue truth diligently, weighing matters fairly and judging according to righteousness rather than appearances. Clarity of judgment protects both justice and relationships within the community.

🩵 REFLECTION

In a fast-moving world, people are often pressured to form opinions quickly. News spreads rapidly, emotions rise easily, and assumptions can take the place of careful examination. Scripture teaches that wisdom requires patience. A righteous person listens before speaking, gathers facts before reaching conclusions, and seeks understanding before rendering judgment.

Consider whether your judgments are shaped by truth and discernment or by first impressions, emotions, and incomplete information. Clarity comes when we slow down, seek wisdom, and allow YHVH’s instruction to guide our decisions.

👐 PRAYER

Abba YHVH, teach us to judge with wisdom, fairness, and discernment. Guard us from hasty conclusions, prejudice, and assumptions that distort truth. Help us to listen carefully, seek understanding, and act with integrity in every matter. Let our decisions reflect Your righteousness and bring peace, justice, and truth into our relationships and communities.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
