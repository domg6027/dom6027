📨 DAVAR LECHEM, Yom Shlishi, Shlishi 22, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

⭐ THEME

Justice – honoring order

📘 READING

“For Elohim is not Elohim of confusion but of peace, as in all the assemblies of the set-apart ones.”
- Qorintiyim Aleph (1 Corinthians) 14:33

“Let all be done decently and in order.”
- Qorintiyim Aleph (1 Corinthians) 14:40

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH is a Creator of order, purpose, and righteousness. Throughout Scripture, He establishes boundaries, responsibilities, and structures that promote peace, justice, and stability. Honoring order is therefore an expression of honoring the wisdom and authority of YHVH.

Justice flourishes where order is respected. When responsibilities are fulfilled, agreements are honored, and people act with integrity, communities are strengthened and conflict is reduced. The people of YHVH are called to be examples of disciplined and orderly living.

🩵 REFLECTION

Disorder often begins with small acts of neglect, carelessness, or disregard for responsibility. Over time, these habits can create confusion, conflict, and instability. By contrast, a life ordered according to the principles of YHVH promotes peace, trustworthiness, and effectiveness.

Consider the areas of your life where greater order may be needed. This may involve managing responsibilities faithfully, keeping commitments, communicating clearly, or maintaining discipline in daily habits. Honoring order is not merely about efficiency; it is about reflecting the character of YHVH in practical ways.

👐 PRAYER

Abba YHVH, thank You for being an Elohim of peace, wisdom, and order. Teach us to honor the structures, responsibilities, and principles that You have established. Help us to walk with discipline, integrity, and faithfulness in every area of life. Guard us from confusion, negligence, and disorder, and guide us in paths that promote justice, peace, and righteousness. Let our lives reflect Your character and bring honor to Your Name.

Baruch YHVH. Emet.

Shalom until tomorrow.
