📨 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Rishon, Sheni 28, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mishpacha!

⭐ THEME

Diligence in judgment

📘 READING

"Be diligent to present yourself approved to Elohim, a worker who does not need to be ashamed, rightly dividing the Word of truth."
- 2 Timothy 2:15

"Do not pervert justice; do not show partiality; and do not accept a bribe, for a bribe blinds the eyes of the wise and subverts the cause of the righteous."
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 16:19

"Learn to do good; seek justice, correct oppression; bring justice to the fatherless, plead the widow’s cause."
- Yeshayahu (Isaiah) 1:17

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH calls His people to exercise diligence and integrity in all judgment and decision-making. Judgments must be fair, impartial, and thorough, reflecting His righteousness and wisdom. Laziness, haste, or partiality in judgment harms both the community and covenant life.

True diligence is not merely about speed but about faithfulness to truth, justice, and equity in every circumstance.

🩵 REFLECTION

Are your decisions, whether in family, work, or community, guided by diligence and fairness? Do you take time to consider consequences, uphold integrity, and act according to YHVH’s principles?

Today is a call to carefully examine all matters before you, ensuring that your judgment brings peace, fairness, and righteousness, rather than haste, error, or partiality.

🌾 OMER 44

Today is Day 44 of the Omer. Each day of counting deepens our discipline, responsibility, and alignment with the ways of YHVH.

Diligence in judgment is a spiritual exercise, refining our hearts and minds to reflect His justice and truth in every decision.

👐 PRAYER

Avinu Malkeinu, grant us wisdom and diligence in all judgments. Guard our hearts from haste, partiality, or neglect. May we always act with integrity, fairness, and faithfulness in every decision, reflecting Your righteousness to those around us.

Baruch YHVH.

Aaronitic Blessing

Shalom until tomorrow.
