📰 DAVAR LECHEM – Yom Rishon, Sheni 21, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mishpacha!

⭐ THEME

Prohibition of false measures

📘 READING

"Do not have in your bag differing weights, a large and a small. Do not have in your house differing measures, a large and a small. You shall have honest scales and honest weights."
- Devarim (Deuteronomy) 25:13-15

"Dishonest scales are an abomination to YHVH, but a just weight is His delight."
- Mishlei (Proverbs) 11:1

"Learn to do good; seek justice, correct oppression; bring justice to the fatherless, plead the widow’s cause."
- Yeshayahu (Isaiah) 1:17

"Whatever you do, do it all for the glory of YHVH."
- 1 Korintiërs (1 Corinthians) 10:31

🟫 MITZVOT FOCUS

YHVH commands us to maintain honest measures, whether in business, trade, or daily dealings. Using false scales, weights, or measures undermines trust and violates His Torah.

Integrity in commerce and fairness in all actions are not optional but a reflection of obedience and covenant life.

🩵 REFLECTION

Where might we be tempted to compromise honesty for gain? False measures are not only physical but also metaphorical—our words, promises, and judgments must all be accurate and true.

Today is a call to examine every area where integrity might be compromised and to realign with YHVH’s standard of honesty.

🌾 OMER 37

Today is Day 37 of the Omer. Each day of this counting invites us into greater refinement, teaching us discipline, accountability, and faithfulness. Honest measures remind us that spiritual and physical integrity must align.

👐 PRAYER

Avinu Malkeinu, teach us to walk in honesty and fairness. Help us to measure all our words, actions, and decisions according to Your truth. Protect our hearts from deceit, and establish us in integrity, that we may honour You in every interaction and responsibility.

Baruch YHVH.

Aaronitic Blessing

Shalom until tomorrow.
