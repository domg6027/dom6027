SHOFAR BLAST


🌿 SPECIAL SHABBAT — (LATE) PESACH SHENI, Sheni 26, 6028 AA

Shalom, Mitspacha!

Today we continue to honour Pesach Sheni – the appointed provision for those delayed or unable to keep Pesach at its earlier time. Let us enter this Shabbat with hearts ready to reflect on restoration, mercy, and faithfulness.

⭐ THEME

Justice – stability of possession / Redemption by blood

🤲🏻 Opening Prayer

Avinu Malkeinu, guide us as we seek justice in all matters of possession and life. Strengthen our hearts to trust Your provision, act with integrity, and remember the redemption You have provided through the Pesach Lamb. Prepare us for the observance of this late Pesach Sheni with sincere devotion and reverent joy.

Baruch YHVH.

🍷 COMMUNION

As we partake in communion, let us remember Yahusha HaMashiach, our Pesach Lamb, whose blood secures redemption and reconciliation. May this cup remind us of covenant renewal, forgiveness, and the faithfulness of YHVH to His people.

📖 MESSAGE

Pesach Sheni stands as a reminder that delay is not rejection. Those unable to celebrate at the appointed time were given an opportunity to participate fully in remembrance and covenant obedience.

This year, through exhaustion and calendar confusion, the observance itself is delayed. Yet even in this, YHVH’s provision is evident – a tangible expression of mercy, restoration, and the stability of His promises.

Justice in our possessions, our dealings, and our stewardship mirrors the faithfulness YHVH demonstrates. Redemption through blood assures us that stability is possible even when timing seems late or circumstances challenging.

Reflect this morning on how you can maintain stability in your responsibilities, trust in His provision, and uphold integrity in all dealings.

🤲🏻 Closing Prayer

Avinu Malkeinu, help us walk in justice and integrity. May we maintain trust in Your provision and live in the redemption secured through the Pesach Lamb. Strengthen our hearts, prepare our homes and tables, and draw us into full participation in this late Pesach Sheni with humility, thanksgiving, and faithfulness.

Baruch YHVH.
