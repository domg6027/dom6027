Responsibility for anotherâ€™s property

The Torah teaches that when something is entrusted to us, we become responsible for its care. Trust creates obligation. Whether property, resources, or even influence, what belongs to another must be guarded with diligence.

Responsibility is not casual stewardship. It requires attentiveness, honesty, and accountability. To neglect what has been placed in our hands is to fail both our neighbor and YHVH.

Justice - damages and liability

The Torah establishes clear principles for damages. When harm is caused - whether intentional or careless - restitution must follow. Justice restores balance.

Liability teaches restraint. It reminds us that our actions have consequences beyond ourselves. True righteousness includes foresight, responsibility, and willingness to repair what we damage.

As we enter Shabbat, these themes unite: guard what is entrusted, and repair what is harmed. Covenant living is built on trust and justice.
