📜 QARA’ EL YHVH — DAILY PRAYER GUIDE

Calling Upon YHVH - Ordered Paleo-Messianic Abrahamic Prayer

Introduction to QARA Prayer

Within Derech Olam Mishkan Int'l, the Ancient Path of ordered remembrance, six appointed daily QARA prayers structure the day around worship, submission, gratitude, repentance, intercession, and covenant remembrance.

Three prayers are regarded as compulsory daily covenant prayers, while three are commendable devotional prayers. Together they restore rhythm, discipline, remembrance, and continual awareness of YHVH.

Meaning of QARA:
QARA means “to call out,” “to proclaim,” “to cry unto,” or “to invoke.” It reflects vocal prayer, proclamation, worship, petition, and covenant remembrance before YHVH.

Daily Prayer Structure

  • Qara Ashmoret - Night Watch Prayer (~ Tahajjud)
  • Qara Boker - Morning Prayer (~ Fajr)
  • Qara Natsah - Noon Prayer (~ Dhuhr)
  • Qara Tzohorayim - Mid-Afternoon Prayer (~ 'Asr)
  • Qara Nesheph - Evening Prayer (~ Maghrib)
  • Qara Erev - Late Evening Prayer (~ 'Isha)

Appointed Rakah Structure

  • Ashmoret: 2 rakahot
  • Boker: 4 rakahot
  • Natsah: 4 rakahot
  • Tzohorayim: 2 rakahot
  • Nesheph: 3 rakahot
  • Erev: 2 rakahot

Universal Changeover Call

La'YHVH! (“Unto YHVH!”) is proclaimed:

  • At the beginning of the prayer
  • At the beginning of every rakah
  • Before every bodily change of position
  • Between completion movements within each rakah

Opening Declaration

Hear, O Yisra'el, YHVH is our Elohim, YHVH is ONE.

Universal Intention

I set my intention to stand before You, YHVH Elohim, to remember You, to give thanks to You, and to submit myself to Your will alone. Accept my prayer and direct my heart in truth.

Opening Recitation

Search me, O Ĕl, and know my heart; try me, and know my thoughts; and see if there is any wicked way in me, and lead me in the Way everlasting.

After the opening recitation, the appointed text for the current rakah is proclaimed.

Upon completion of the rakah text, proclaim La'YHVH!, raise the hands with palms upward, and proclaim: Hallelu-YHVH (three times).

Universal Completion

  1. Full prostration: “Baruch YHVH.” (three times)
  2. Sitting upright: “Todah va Yadah.” (three times)
  3. Second prostration: “Baruch YHVH.” (three times)

Closing Shema

Shema Yisra'el, YHVH Eloheinu, YHVH Echad!

Closing Greeting

Turn gently to the right and say: Shalom Aleichem.

Turn gently to the left and repeat: Shalom Aleichem.

Second Rakah - Tehillim 91

Tehillim (Psalms) 91

1 He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High shall abide under the shadow of Shaddai.

2 I will say of YHVH, “He is my refuge and my fortress; my Elohim, in whom I trust.”

3 Surely He shall deliver you from the snare of the fowler and from the destructive pestilence.

4 He shall cover you with His feathers, and under His wings you shall trust; His truth shall be your shield and armor.

5 You shall not fear the terror by night, nor the arrow that flies by day,

6 nor the pestilence that walks in darkness, nor the destruction that wastes at noonday.

7 A thousand may fall at your side, and ten thousand at your right hand; but it shall not come near you.

8 Only with your eyes shall you behold and see the reward of the wicked.

9 Because you have made YHVH, who is my refuge, even the Most High, your dwelling place,

10 no evil shall befall you, nor shall any plague come near your tent, 11 For He shall give His angels charge over you, to guard you in all your ways.

12 They shall bear you up in their hands, lest you strike your foot against a stone.

13 You shall tread upon the lion and the cobra; the young lion and the serpent you shall trample underfoot.

14 Because he has set his love upon Me, therefore I will deliver him; I will set him on high, because he has known My Name.

15 He shall call upon Me, and I will answer him; I will be with him in trouble; I will deliver him and honor him.

16 With long life I will satisfy him, and show him My salvation.

Second Rakah - Adon’s Prayer

Our Father who is in the heavens,
let Your Name be set-apart.
Let Your Kingdom come.
Let Your desire be done on earth as it is in heaven.

Give us today our daily bread.
And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors.

but deliver us from the wicked one.

For Yours is the Kingdom, the power, and the esteem forever.
